The shell is all the tool you need for building simple agents. It is also a methodology for producing reliable knowledge with agents. Unix primitives provide a verifiable substrate for agent architectures, enabling decomposition of reasoning into inspectable, refutable components. The constraints of composability force clarity, and clarity enables verification. Two control models (script-driven and LLM-driven) compose over the same primitives, with trust determined by inspectability, security determined by who controls the workflow, and scalability determined by composability.
