# c02: The algorithm's "proposing" maps to real-world "asking"

The article's central move: reframing the gender-neutral algorithm as "asker-optimal, askee-pessimal." This requires that (a) proposing ≈ asking, (b) preferences are rankable, and (c) matching is sequential.

This is the load-bearing bridge between the proven math and the life-advice conclusion. Every real-world claim in the article passes through this compound. If the mapping is weak, the math — however airtight — doesn't support the life advice.

The mapping is intuitive but each dependency introduces assumptions the algorithm doesn't require.
