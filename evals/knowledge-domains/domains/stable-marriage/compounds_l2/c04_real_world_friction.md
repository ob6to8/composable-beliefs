# c04: Real-world friction modifies the algorithm's conclusions

Social sanctions, psychological costs of rejection, commitment stickiness, and investment effects all introduce friction the algorithm assumes away. These frictions don't necessarily reverse the conclusion but they bound it — the advantage of asking is real but not absolute, and in some contexts the costs may exceed the benefits.

Notably, p13 (stickiness improves outcomes) actively undermines the algorithm's notion of "optimal" by suggesting that match quality is partly endogenous to commitment.
