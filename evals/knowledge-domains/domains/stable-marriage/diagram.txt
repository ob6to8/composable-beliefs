LAYER 1 (Primitives)
────────────────────

  MATH (proven, not contestable):
  [p01] Gale-Shapley always terminates in stable matching
  [p02] Result is proposer-optimal (best in ANY stable matching)
  [p03] Result is simultaneously acceptor-pessimal
  [p04] No external optimizer can improve any proposer's outcome

  BRIDGE (maps math to reality, highly contestable):
  [p05] "Proposing" in algorithm maps to "asking" in real life
  [p06] Real-world preferences can be approximated as rankable
  [p07] Real-world matching is iterative and sequential

  EMPIRICAL (observable, moderately contestable):
  [p08] Initiators select from full universe; passive select from who approaches
  [p09] Cold-asking sometimes produces results that wouldn't otherwise exist

  CAVEATS (the article's own qualifications):
  [p10] Social sanctions exist for asking above your station
  [p11] Rejection carries real psychological costs
  [p12] Commitments are sticky due to transaction costs
  [p13] Stickiness can improve outcomes beyond theoretical optimum
