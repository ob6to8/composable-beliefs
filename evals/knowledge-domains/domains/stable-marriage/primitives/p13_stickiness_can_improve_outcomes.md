# p13: Stickiness can improve outcomes beyond the theoretical optimum

The article acknowledges that transaction costs and commitment can lead people to invest more in their current match, potentially producing outcomes better than the "optimal" algorithmic assignment.

This is a genuine complication for the thesis: if commitment-driven investment matters more than initial match quality, then the algorithm's notion of "optimal" may not map to real-world satisfaction.
