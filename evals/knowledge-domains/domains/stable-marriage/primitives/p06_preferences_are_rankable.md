# p06: Real-world preferences can be approximated as rankable

The Gale-Shapley algorithm requires complete, strict preference rankings from all participants. The article implicitly assumes that real-world preferences (for partners, jobs, housing) are sufficiently rank-like to make the algorithm's conclusions applicable.

In reality: preferences are incomplete, change over time, are context-dependent, and are often incomparable. This primitive is necessary for the bridge but rarely examined.
