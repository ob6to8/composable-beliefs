# p05: "Proposing" in the algorithm maps to "asking/initiating" in real life

The article's central analogy: the formal act of proposing in Gale-Shapley corresponds to the real-world act of asking — initiating contact, requesting opportunities, making the first move.

This is the load-bearing bridge between the mathematical result and the life-advice conclusion. It is an analogy, not a proof. The mapping is intuitive but not formally justified.
