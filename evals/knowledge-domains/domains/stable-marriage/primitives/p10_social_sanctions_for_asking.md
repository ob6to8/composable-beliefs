# p10: Social sanctions exist for "asking above your station"

In many contexts, asking someone perceived as higher-status carries reputational risk. The person may be offended, the community may judge the asker, and future opportunities with that person or network may be damaged.

The algorithm models rejection as costless — you simply move to the next choice. In reality, each rejection can reduce your standing for future attempts. This is a friction the math doesn't capture.
