# p07: Real-world matching is iterative and sequential, like the algorithm

The algorithm proceeds in rounds: propose, get rejected, propose to next choice. The article assumes real-world matching has a similar sequential structure — you ask one person/employer/opportunity, get a result, then potentially ask another.

This is partially true (job applications, dating) but real-world matching often involves parallel processes, incomplete information about who's available, and no clear "rounds."
